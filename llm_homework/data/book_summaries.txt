## Title: 1984
A dystopian tale by George Orwell, where society is under constant surveillance by an authoritarian regime led by Big Brother. Citizens are monitored, and even thoughts are policed through strict control mechanisms. Winston Smith attempts to break free and discover the truth. The novel explores the dangers of censorship, propaganda, and psychological control. Themes: totalitarianism, freedom, resistance, surveillance.

## Title: The Hobbit
Bilbo Baggins, a timid hobbit, is drawn into an unexpected adventure with a band of dwarves to reclaim their treasure from the dragon Smaug. On the journey, Bilbo faces trolls, goblins, and discovers the One Ring. He gradually transforms into a brave and clever hero. The story blends mythical creatures and rich fantasy landscapes with personal growth. Themes: adventure, courage, transformation, friendship.

## Title: To Kill a Mockingbird
Set in the racially segregated South, this novel follows Scout Finch as her father, Atticus, defends a Black man falsely accused of assault. Through her eyes, we see a child's perspective on justice, prejudice, and moral development. The town's deep-rooted biases are laid bare. The book contrasts innocence with injustice in a powerful coming-of-age narrative. Themes: racism, justice, innocence, integrity.

## Title: Harry Potter and the Sorcerer’s Stone
Harry learns on his 11th birthday that he is a wizard and is whisked away to Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. There, he finds friends, uncovers secrets about his parents, and faces a dark magical threat. The magical world is rich and captivating. Through trials, he begins to define himself beyond the “Boy Who Lived” title. Themes: magic, friendship, bravery, destiny.

## Title: The Great Gatsby
Nick Carraway moves to West Egg and meets the enigmatic millionaire Jay Gatsby, who is obsessed with rekindling a past romance with Daisy Buchanan. Lavish parties mask loneliness and moral decay. As Gatsby's dream unravels, the hollowness of wealth and status becomes clear. The novel paints a grim picture of the American Dream. Themes: illusion, love, wealth, disillusionment.

## Title: The Catcher in the Rye
Holden Caulfield narrates a few days in his life after being expelled from school, wandering around New York City. He reflects on adulthood, grief, and his fading innocence. His internal monologue exposes deep alienation and a longing to protect purity. The book captures the teenage struggle against a conformist world. Themes: alienation, adolescence, identity, mental health.

## Title: Pride and Prejudice
Elizabeth Bennet navigates social pressures and misunderstandings in 19th-century England while dealing with her evolving feelings for the proud Mr. Darcy. The novel critiques class structures and the limited roles of women. Through wit and self-reflection, love triumphs over prejudice. The characters grow emotionally and intellectually. Themes: social class, love, pride, personal growth.

## Title: The Alchemist
Santiago, a shepherd, dreams of finding treasure near the Egyptian pyramids and embarks on a journey of spiritual discovery. Along the way, he meets mentors who guide him toward understanding his true self. The story encourages listening to one’s heart and following dreams. It’s a metaphor-laden narrative with universal appeal. Themes: purpose, destiny, personal legend, transformation.

## Title: Animal Farm
A group of animals overthrows their human farmer, hoping to build a fair society. But power soon corrupts, and the pigs take control, becoming as oppressive as the humans. The revolution’s ideals fade into tyranny. Orwell critiques political manipulation and the betrayal of revolutions. The story is a sharp allegory of real-world history. Themes: power, equality, corruption, propaganda.

## Title: The Chronicles of Narnia: The Lion, the Witch and the Wardrobe
Four siblings stumble through a wardrobe into Narnia, a magical land frozen in eternal winter by the White Witch. They join Aslan, a noble lion, in the battle to restore peace and freedom. The story weaves themes of sacrifice, faith, and redemption. Rich symbolism underlies this fantastical adventure. Themes: good vs evil, belief, family, redemption.
